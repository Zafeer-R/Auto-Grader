# S03: Data Table Grading (R1, R2, R3)

**Goal:** Students fill data tables R1, R2, R3 as editable grids; submission grades R2/R3 per-cell with consistency flags derived from the student's own R1 data
**Demo:** Student fills R1 raw measurements, R2 statistics, R3 volume/density; submits; sees per-cell ✓/✗ with consistency warnings (flag-only, no deduction per D003)

## Design (from Lab01_Report.docx source)

- **R1** (Trial 1-5 × Length/Width/Thickness/Mass): raw student-collected data — no ground truth exists. Graded for completeness only (0 pts; TA verifies via Check Box #2 "Data sets checked").
- **R2** [12 pts] (Mean/SD/SEOM/Error × 4 measurements, "to 0.001 cm and 0.01 g"): per-cell grading. Mean cells graded against nominal tile dimensions (wide tolerance, matching S02 pattern); SD/SEOM/Error cells have no meaningful nominal — graded for parseable value at required precision.
- **R3** [12 pts] (Mean/Highest/Lowest/Uncertainty × Volume/Density, "to 0.01 cm³ and 0.01 g/cm³"): same policy — volume/density means have nominal anchors (Al tile ≈ 8.26 cm³, 2.70 g/cm³); highest/lowest/uncertainty graded for precision+completeness.
- **Consistency checks (D003, flag-only):** recompute expected R2 from student's R1 (mean, sample SD, SEOM=SD/√5, error=max(SEOM, instrument uncertainty)) and expected R3 from those stats (V=LWT, min/max propagation, unc=(hi−lo)/2). Cells that disagree with the student's own data get a ⚠ flag for TA review — never a deduction.
- **Cell grading policy lives in the answer key:** cell with `expected`+`tolerance` → numerical grading; cell without → completeness+precision grading. Instructor can tune per cell.
- **Points correction:** meas_balance/meas_calipers drop from 1 pt to 0 pt (docx allocates them none; restores exact 100-pt budget: 10 safety + 9 Q1 + 15 Q2 + 12 Q3 + 10 CB1 + 10 CB2 + 24 tables + 10 Q4 = 100). Supersedes implicit S02 choice — recorded in DECISIONS.md.

## Must-Haves
